# PRODUCT.md — Paramount.com (target)

## Register

`brand` — corporate brand/marketing site for a global house of entertainment brands. Identity and reputation, not transactional UI.

## Users

- **Investors & financial press** — routed to investor relations, results, leadership.
- **Job seekers** — the careers track ("SHAPE THE FUTURE / JOIN US") is a primary CTA.
- **Press & media** — News, Press Releases.
- **Consumers / fans** — addressed through IP showcase and the brand portfolio, then funneled to consumer brands (Paramount+, Pluto TV…).

## Product Purpose

Present Paramount — post-Skydance merger — as one unified house of premium entertainment brands. A portfolio statement: "we are the company behind the entertainment you already love." Aggregate ~12 consumer brands under one corporate identity and route distinct audiences to their destinations.

## Brand Personality

Bold, confident, pop-culture-forward, unafraid of scale. **Display-typography signature** (Paramount Vista Sans at 112–160px) + **flat saturated color blocking** (midnight navy ↔ electric blue) with first-party IP photography carrying the emotion. Declarative, celebratory voice in three-word statements. Corporate but never sterile — it fronts its joy (SpongeBob, Star Trek, RuPaul), not its org chart.

Maps to the **`kinetic-display`** motion register: enormous typography and rotating IP imagery as the signature — type and imagery in motion, not physics or data.

## Anti-references

- Sterile corporate-holding-company sites (press-release walls, boardroom stock photography).
- Generic SaaS/landing-page template (centered hero, stock photo, double CTA in primary blue).
- Streaming-app content-grid clutter (this is the corporate parent, not the Paramount+ app).
- Dark-mode-with-neon-gradient tech aesthetic; glassmorphism.

## Design Principles

1. **The IP is the hero.** Real shows and characters carry the emotion; the corporate message frames them.
2. **Say it big, say it short.** Display type at 112–160px; three-word statements.
3. **Flat, saturated color blocking.** Navy and electric blue do the structural work; no shadows, no gradients.
4. **One portfolio, many brands.** The brand grid is the load-bearing "what we are" surface — but with hierarchy, not an equal logo wall.
