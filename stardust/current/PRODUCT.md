# PRODUCT.md — Paramount.com (current state)

## Register

`brand` — a corporate brand/marketing site for a global media holding company. Its job is identity and reputation, not transactional product UI. Inferred from the landing-page structure, the celebratory single-idea headlines, and the absence of any tool/dashboard surface.

## Users

- **Investors & financial press** — routed to `ir.paramount.com`, quarterly results, board/leadership.
- **Job seekers** — the "SHAPE THE FUTURE / JOIN US" careers band and the Careers nav pillar are a primary first-viewport-adjacent CTA.
- **Press & media** — News and Press Releases pillars.
- **Consumers / fans** — implicitly addressed through the IP showcase (SpongeBob, Star Trek, RuPaul, NCIS) and the brand-portfolio grid, though they are usually sent onward to the consumer brands (Paramount+, Pluto TV, etc.).

## Product Purpose

Present Paramount — post-Skydance merger — as one unified house of premium entertainment brands. The site is a portfolio statement: "we are the company behind the entertainment you already love." It aggregates ~12 consumer brands under one corporate identity and funnels distinct audiences (investors, talent, press) to their destinations.

## Brand Personality

Bold, confident, pop-culture-forward, and unafraid of scale. The design leans on **oversized editorial display type** and **flat, saturated color blocking** (midnight navy ↔ electric blue) with celebrity/IP photography doing the emotional work. The voice is declarative and celebratory — three-word statements ("WE ARE", "WHAT YOU LOVE", "SHAPE THE FUTURE") rather than paragraphs. It is corporate but never sterile: SpongeBob mid-jump on the homepage signals a holding company that fronts its joy, not its org chart.

Maps to the **`kinetic-display`** motion register (per prototype/reference/motion-registers.md): the brand's signature is enormous typography and a rotating IP carousel — type and imagery in motion, not physics or data.

## Anti-references

- Sterile corporate-holding-company sites (walls of press-release text, stock-photo boardrooms, no IP visible).
- Generic SaaS/landing-page template (centered hero, stock photo, double CTA in primary blue).
- Streaming-service UI clutter (Paramount.com is the corporate parent, not the Paramount+ app — it must not look like a content carousel grid you scroll forever).
- Dark-mode-with-neon-gradient tech aesthetic.

## Design Principles

1. **The IP is the hero.** Real shows and characters carry the emotion; the corporate message frames them.
2. **Say it big, say it short.** Display type at 112–160px; three-word statements.
3. **Flat, saturated color blocking.** Navy and electric blue do the structural work; no shadows, no gradients.
4. **One portfolio, many brands.** The 12-tile grid is the load-bearing "what we are" surface.
