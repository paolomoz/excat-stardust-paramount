Tensions surfaced

dated-pattern
The brand grid is an undifferentiated 4×3 wall of equal tiles. Twelve same-size portrait tiles with no hierarchy, weighting, or editorial rhythm reads as a 2018 "logo wall" pattern. Paramount+ (the flagship) gets the same visual weight as a sub-brand.
ia-clutter
The hero says "WE ARE" but never lands the proposition. Between the rotating IP, the script wordmark, the "WE ARE" SVG and "MORE OF WHAT YOU LOVE", the first viewport carries four competing typographic statements with no single focal claim.
contrast-or-density
Flat two-field color is confident but monotone over a full scroll. Navy → blue → navy → navy with no tonal relief or texture means the page has one dynamic range; the careers band and footer blur together.
cliché
The careers CTA is a generic single-portrait + headline band. "SHAPE THE FUTURE / JOIN US" with one smiling employee on navy is the default corporate-careers module; it doesn't capitalize on the IP energy the rest of the page has.
missed-opportunity
The IP carousel is the best asset and it's trapped in the hero. The rotating SpongeBob/Star Trek/RuPaul imagery is the most alive thing on the site, but it only appears once, auto-rotating, behind a wordmark — the motion and star power never carry into the rest of the page.
Strengths to preserve: a genuinely distinctive proprietary display face, a fearless saturated palette, real IP photography (not stock), and a flat shadowless discipline that already feels modern. The uplift keeps all of these (Mode A: palette + typography pinned) and fixes the tensions above.
